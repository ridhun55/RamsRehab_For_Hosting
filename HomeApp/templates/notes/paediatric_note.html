Autism spectrum disorders

- Autism spectrum disorder is a condition related to brain development
- impacts how a person perceives and socializes with others
- causing problems in social interaction and communication.
- limited and repetitive patterns of behavior.
- Autism spectrum disorder begins in early childhood
- signs of autism spectrum disorder in early infancy, such as reduced eye contact, lack of response to their name or indifference to caregivers
- Some children have high IQ while some have poor IQ and learning defects

Therapeutic Exercise

- Milestone training
- Functional reeducation
- Brock string exercises for visual accomodation
- Proprioception training
- Strength training
- Flexibility exercises

Sensory integration disorders

- Sensory integration dysfunction is when a child is not able to manage the information coming in through their senses.
- This may interfere with learning, playing, social interaction, and completing daily activities of life.

Therapeutic Exercise

- Milestone training
- Play therapy
- Functional reeducation
- Proprioceptive training
- Brock string exercises for visual accomodation
- Balance training
- Coordination training
- Aerobic exercises
- Spider cage therapy

Therapy 3

Cerebral palsy

- Non progressive damage to the immature brain
- Damage can occure during pregnancy period, during birth or after birth
- Motor&sensory disturbances
- Presents Hypotonic or hypertonic muscle tone according to the type of disease
- Speech, intellectual, cognitive and behavioral defects
- Impaired posture and gate
- Associated orthopedic abnormalities

Therapeutic Exercise

- Milestone training
- Functional reeducation
- Stretching
- Balance training
- Coordination training
- Brock string exercise for visual accomodation
- Gait training
- Aerobic exercises
- Peg board exercises
- Post isometric relaxation exercises
